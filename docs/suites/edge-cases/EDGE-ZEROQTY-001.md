## EDGE-ZEROQTY-001 — A zero-quantity line on a sales order is rejected or explicitly informational

```yaml
id: EDGE-ZEROQTY-001
title: A sales order line with quantity zero is either rejected or treated as informational with no GL impact
goal: |
  Verify that a sales order line entered with quantity = 0 either is
  rejected at entry, or is permitted only as an informational marker
  that does not post to revenue, AR, or inventory.
roles:
  - Order Entry
  - Controller
capabilities:
  - CAP-O2C-SO
  - CAP-O2C-INVOICE
preconditions:
  - At least one customer.
  - At least one part.
steps:
  - n: 1
    action: |
      Create a sales order with one line at quantity = 0, unit price
      $50.
    expected: |
      The application either:
      (a) rejects the line with a "quantity must be positive" message,
      OR
      (b) accepts the line and clearly labels it as informational
      (e.g., "no charge").
      The behavior is documented.
  - n: 2
    action: |
      If accepted, attempt to ship and invoice. Inspect GL postings.
    expected: |
      No revenue, no AR, no inventory movement is generated by the
      zero line. The order's net is unchanged.
  - n: 3
    action: |
      Run the order register / sales report.
    expected: |
      The zero-quantity line either does not appear, or appears with a
      clear $0 marker that does not feed totals.
expected_overall: |
  Zero-quantity lines do not corrupt revenue, AR, or inventory.
pass_criteria: |
  Behavior matches one of the documented policies AND no nonzero
  posting results from a zero quantity.
why_this_matters: |
  A zero-quantity line that nonetheless triggers GL postings — even
  zero-amount ones — clutters the audit trail and confuses
  reconciliation. The system has to be deliberate about it.
est_minutes: 6
```
